Nelson Mandela

“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.”

  • Mandela was born on July 18, 1918, in the village of Mvezo, South Africa.
  • He was given the name "Rolihlahla," which means "troublemaker" in his Xhosa language.
  • Mandela became involved in anti-apartheid activism, leading to his involvement with the African National Congress (ANC).
  • He was arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment in 1962 for his opposition to apartheid policies.
  • Mandela spent 27 years in prison, much of it on Robben Island, before being released in 1990.
  • He played a leading role in negotiating an end to apartheid and transitioning South Africa to majority rule.
  • Mandela became South Africa's first black president in 1994, serving until 1999.
  • He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 for his efforts to dismantle apartheid peacefully.
  • Mandela is celebrated worldwide for his advocacy of reconciliation and forgiveness.
  • He passed away on December 5, 2013, at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of courage and resilience.
